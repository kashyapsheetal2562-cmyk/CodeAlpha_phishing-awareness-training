# 🛡️ Phishing Awareness Quiz

Test your knowledge about phishing attacks, fake websites, and social engineering tactics.  
Choose the correct answer for each question.

---

## 1. Suspicious Lottery Email
You receive an email claiming you won a lottery and asking for your bank details.  
- A) Legitimate offer  
- B) Phishing attempt ✅

---

## 2. Fake Website URL
The website URL looks like `paypa1.com` instead of `paypal.com`.  
- A) Safe site  
- B) Fake site ✅

---

## 3. Urgent Action Request
An email says: *“Your account will be locked in 24 hours unless you click this link.”*  
- A) Normal security procedure  
- B) Phishing tactic ✅

---

## 4. Unexpected Attachment
You get an email from an unknown sender with a file named `invoice.pdf.exe`.  
- A) Safe to open  
- B) Dangerous attachment ✅

---

## 5. Social Engineering Trick
An attacker pretends to be your manager and asks for confidential files.  
- A) Normal request  
- B) Social engineering attack ✅

---

## 6. Best Practice
Which is the safest way to verify a suspicious email?  
- A) Click the link to check  
- B) Contact the sender directly ✅

---

## 7. HTTPS vs HTTP
A legitimate banking site should always use:  
- A) `http://`  
- B) `https://` ✅

## 8. Grammar & Spelling Errors
Phishing emails often contain:  
- A) Perfect grammar  
- B) Poor spelling and grammar ✅

---

## 9. Hovering Over Links
Before clicking a link in an email, you should:  
- A) Hover over it to see the real URL ✅  
- B) Click immediately

---

## 10. Reporting Phishing

- A) Delete it silently  
- B) Report it to IT/security team ✅

## 📊 Scoring

- 5–7 correct → Good, but review best practices
